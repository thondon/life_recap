2024 was not a year of perfection, but a year of proof.

This year, someone challenged me to live life the way they did—to struggle the way they did. They said that if I was truly capable, I should be able to do anything, no matter the circumstances.

So I chose to give up one full year to become a real high school student. I stayed up late studying, completed assignments endlessly, and lived the routine that society expects from someone at that stage of life. I did my best—not by my own standards, but by the standards of those who believe this is what a high school student should be.

I didn’t do this to prove superiority. I did it to understand. And through living it, I earned the right to speak—not from imagination, but from experience.

This challenge is designed to select suitable participants, not to eliminate unsuitable ones, since at the beginning we did not know who would be participating.

Award Recipients:
Even though no one receives the award in 2025, don’t be discouraged. In the coming years, I will always continue to look for opportunities. You just won’t know when—and no one else will either—because those who receive it must always possess the required qualities within themselves.

This challenge is meant to select those who have an intense desire to learn, because they have already learned the value of time that has been wasted. You will receive the message:

“I got very good exam scores even without studying beforehand, and I was casually playing games too.”

I know very well that you might think I’m bragging, which is normal. But what I’m looking for are responses such as:

  • “How did you do it?”
  • “How does it work?”
  • “Are there any tips?”
  • Or anything else that shows you are ready to learn from everything.

Why this question?
Because simply giving someone my support would be easy—but how ready are they to truly become capable? I understand that high school life is often too fun to worry about where it will lead. Still, I want to help those who think:

“I don’t have much time left. I need to study and also be happy with the people I love. How can I make the best use of the time I have?”

They have already realized that once in a lifetime, time that is lost cannot be recovered, and that what remains must be used to the fullest—until the lives of those they love become greater than their own pride.

— Thondon
